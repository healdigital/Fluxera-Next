# License Renewal Alerts

Learn how to set up, manage, and respond to license expiration alerts to ensure uninterrupted service and maintain compliance.

## Overview

Fluxera's renewal alert system helps you stay ahead of license expirations by sending timely notifications. This ensures you have enough time to review, budget for, and process renewals before services are interrupted.

## How Renewal Alerts Work

The system automatically monitors all licenses with expiration dates and sends alerts based on configurable thresholds. Alerts are sent through multiple channels to ensure you never miss an important renewal.

## Alert Timeline

### Default Alert Schedule

Fluxera sends alerts at these intervals before expiration:

- **90 days before**: Early planning notification
- **60 days before**: Budget approval reminder
- **30 days before**: First urgent warning
- **14 days before**: Second urgent reminder
- **7 days before**: Critical alert
- **On expiration day**: Final notification
- **After expiration**: Overdue alerts (daily for 7 days)

## Configuring Alert Settings

### Global Alert Settings

To configure system-wide alert settings:

1. Go to **Settings** > **Notifications**
2. Find "License Expiration Alerts" section
3. Configure:
   - Enable/disable alerts
   - Alert thresholds (days before expiration)
   - Notification channels (email, in-app, both)
   - Alert recipients

### Per-License Settings

To customize alerts for specific licenses:

1. Open the license detail page
2. Click **Alert Settings**
3. Configure:
   - Custom alert thresholds
   - Additional recipients
   - Special instructions
   - Escalation rules

### Alert Recipients

Configure who receives alerts:

- **License Owner**: Primary person responsible
- **Finance Team**: For budget approval
- **IT Administrators**: For technical renewals
- **Management**: For high-value licenses
- **Custom Recipients**: Add specific email addresses

## Responding to Alerts

### Alert Actions

When you receive an alert, you can:

1. **Acknowledge**: Mark that you've seen the alert
2. **Snooze**: Temporarily dismiss (will reappear)
3. **Renew**: Process the renewal
4. **Cancel**: Mark license for non-renewal
5. **Escalate**: Forward to another team member

### Renewal Workflow

Follow this workflow when responding to alerts:

#### Step 1: Review (90-60 days before)
- Evaluate if license is still needed
- Check current utilization
- Identify any changes in requirements
- Document decision to renew or cancel

#### Step 2: Budget (60-30 days before)
- Request renewal quote from vendor
- Submit budget approval request
- Get necessary approvals
- Prepare purchase order

#### Step 3: Process (30-7 days before)
- Process payment
- Receive new license key (if applicable)
- Update license information in Fluxera
- Verify renewal is complete

#### Step 4: Confirm (After renewal)
- Update expiration date
- Document renewal in notes
- Verify service continuity
- Dismiss alert

To stop receiving certain alerts:

## Troubleshooting

**Q: I'm not receiving alert emails. Why?**
A: Check your notification settings and email spam folder. Verify your email address is correct in your profile.

**Q: Can I change alert thresholds?**
A: Yes, go to Settings > Notifications to customize when alerts are sent.

**Q: What if I miss an alert?**
A: The system continues sending alerts until acknowledged. Check the Alerts page for any missed notifications.

**Q: Can I delegate alerts to someone else?**
A: Yes, you can assign alerts to other team members or add them as recipients in alert settings.

**Q: How do I stop alerts for a license I'm not renewing?**
A: Mark the license as "Not Renewing" in the license settings. Alerts will stop but the license remains in your inventory.

**Q: Can I get alerts via SMS or Slack?**
A: Currently, alerts are sent via email and in-app notifications. Additional channels may be added in future updates.

## Common Scenarios

### High-Value License Renewal

For expensive or critical licenses:

1. Set custom alert thresholds (start at 90 days)
2. Add multiple recipients including management
3. Create detailed renewal checklist
4. Schedule review meetings
5. Document approval process

### Bulk Renewals

When multiple licenses expire together:

1. Group licenses by vendor
2. Negotiate bulk renewal discount
3. Process renewals together
4. Update all licenses at once
5. Verify all renewals completed

### Trial License Conversion

When trial licenses are expiring:

1. Evaluate trial results
2. Decide to purchase or cancel
3. If purchasing, process as new license
4. If canceling, document reasons
5. Unassign and archive trial license

### Vendor Changes

When switching vendors:

1. Acknowledge expiration alert
2. Mark old license as "Not Renewing"
3. Create new license with new vendor
4. Migrate users/assets to new license
5. Document migration in notes
